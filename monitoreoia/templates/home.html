This is a specialized platform for analysis of Mossbauer spectral based on artificial intelligence (AI) developed by the Solid State group and the Scientific Instrumentation and Microelectronics group of the University of Antioquia. This service connects in real time to the Mossbauer spectrometer located at the Sede de Investigación Universitaria (SIU), which allows us to have personalized remote access to the experiments performed on it.

Solid State
Group

Our group works to develop research in condensed matter and to irrigate its results in teaching, industry and society in general. The Solid State group was created, during the first semester of 1972, by Dr. Fred Ferate, professor of the Physics Department, who promoted the creation of this line of work.

Vision

In the next few years the group will have improved the methods of synthesis of iron oxides, magnetic semiconductor oxides, pure and doped manganese and titanium, which it currently uses, and in this way will be presenting more complete and in-depth studies on their electrical and magnetic properties. Studies on obtaining and characterizing thin films of iron oxides, magnetic semiconductor oxides, titanium dioxide, manganites, thermoelectrics and epitaxial and amorphous titanium nitride will have been improved, and corrosion experiments on such films will have been carried out to deepen the understanding of corrosion, protection and storage processes. New applications of these films will also be sought.... see more.
